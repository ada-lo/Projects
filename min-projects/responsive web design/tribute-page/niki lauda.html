Andreas Nikolaus Lauda

Here's a time line of Niki Lauda's life:

  • 1949-Born and raised in Vienna
  • 1968-Lauda begins his racing career in Formula 1. After initially competing in lower formulas, he makes his F1 debut with March Engineering.
  • 1971-Lauda signs with Ferrari, one of the most prestigious teams in Formula 1, and begins to gain recognition for his consistent performance and racing skills.
  • 1975-Niki Lauda wins his first World Championship driving for Ferrari. His outstanding skill and consistency in the 1975 season make him one of the leading drivers in the sport.
  • 1976- The Terrible Crash and ComebackThe Terrible Crash and Comeback
    • June 20, 1976:Lauda suffers a horrific crash at the German Grand Prix at the Nürburgring. The accident leaves him with severe burns and damage to his lungs.
    • Despite the severity of his injuries, Lauda makes a miraculous recovery and returns to racing just six weeks later at the Italian Grand Prix in Monza.
    • His determination to return so quickly became an iconic moment in sports history
  • 1977-Second World ChampionshipLauda wins his second World Championship in 1977, cementing his place among the all-time greats of Formula 1.
  • 1978-Leaves FerrariLauda leaves Ferrari to join Brabham, a move that shocks many. Despite the change, Lauda continues to perform at a high level in the sport.
  • 1982-Retirement from RacingAfter a challenging year with Brabham and declining results, Niki Lauda retires from Formula 1 for the first time.
  • 1984-Third World ChampionshipNiki Lauda makes a comeback to F1 with McLaren. He wins his third World Championship in a dramatic season where he narrowly beats teammate Alain Prost.
  • 1985-Retires AgainLauda retires once more from active racing, deciding to focus on other aspects of his life.
  • 1988-Becomes a Team PrincipalLauda transitions into a managerial role. He becomes a consultant for Ferrari and later takes over Jaguar's Formula 1 team.
  • 1990s-Business VenturesNiki Lauda becomes a successful entrepreneur, particularly in the aviation business. He founds Lauda Air, an airline company, and later sells it to Austrian Airlines in the 2000s.
  • 2000s-Formula 1 Involvement
    • Lauda continues to remain involved in the F1 world, particularly as an advisor and consultant. He holds significant roles in Honda and Ferrari teams.
    • 2009:Lauda becomes a non-executive chairman of Mercedes-Benz Formula One Team, helping guide the team to eventual success.
  • 2019 – Passing of Niki LaudaMay 20, 2019: Niki Lauda passes away at the age of 70. His death is widely mourned by the motorsport community and beyond. He left a lasting legacy as one of the greatest drivers in the history of Formula 1.

Legacy

  • Niki Lauda is remembered not only for his incredible racing achievements but also for his resilience and courage, especially after his near-fatal accident in 1976.
  • His life story was famously portrayed in the film "Rush" (2013), directed by Ron Howard, which chronicled his rivalry with British driver James Hunt during the 1976 Formula 1 season.
  • He was known as a perfectionist and a true professional, admired for his detailed approach to racing and his ability to bounce back from setbacks.
